Parliament Marks Four Years of Russia's War Against Ukraine with Fresh Support Package

EP10 adopts Ukraine loan, migration reforms, climate framework and human rights resolutions in intensive February sessions

⚡ BREAKING — 2026-03-04T06:13:49.752Z

The European Parliament marked four years since Russia launched its full-scale invasion of Ukraine on February 24 with a landmark resolution (TA-10-2026-0056) calling for sustained European security commitments and a just peace. The resolution, adopted during an intensive series of February plenary sessions, caps a productive legislative period that saw EP10 advance major initiatives across defence, migration, climate, financial governance and human rights — demonstrating the fragmented parliament's capacity to build cross-group coalitions on critical policy domains.

Ukraine: Four Years of War, Renewed Commitment

The centrepiece of the February plenary was the adoption of a comprehensive resolution marking four years of Russia's war of aggression against Ukraine (TA-10-2026-0056), calling for European contributions to a just peace and sustained security. This was reinforced by concrete financial measures: Parliament approved the Ukraine Support Loan regulation for 2026–2027 (TA-10-2026-0035), amended the Ukraine Facility regulation (TA-10-2026-0036), and revised the multiannual financial framework (TA-10-2026-0037) to accommodate additional funding. The combined package signals EP10's determination to maintain long-term strategic support for Kyiv.

Migration and Asylum: Operationalising the Pact

Parliament advanced the implementation of the EU Pact on Migration and Asylum through two significant texts. The establishment of a Union-level list of safe countries of origin (TA-10-2026-0025) and the application of the safe third country concept (TA-10-2026-0026) represent the first concrete legislative steps in operationalising the landmark agreement reached during EP9. These measures required careful coalition building across EPP, S&D and Renew, reflecting the political sensitivity of migration policy in the fragmented tenth parliament.

Climate and Environmental Policy

The adoption of a framework for achieving climate neutrality (TA-10-2026-0031) demonstrates continued legislative momentum on the European Green Deal despite the rightward political shift in EP10. The framework builds on the Climate Law and sets out implementation pathways for the 2050 target, while also addressing concerns about competitiveness raised by industrial policy advocates.

Human Rights and External Affairs

Parliament reinforced its human rights watchdog role with a series of urgency resolutions: the systemic oppression and arbitrary detentions in Iran (TA-10-2026-0046), targeted expulsions of journalists and Christians in Türkiye (TA-10-2026-0047), the post-election crisis in Uganda (TA-10-2026-0045), and the humanitarian situation in Northeast Syria (TA-10-2026-0053). The EU Magnitsky Act framework on addressing impunity through sanctions (TA-10-2026-0015) strengthens the EU's capacity to respond to human rights violations globally.

Financial Governance and ECB Oversight

Parliament approved the appointment of the Vice-Chair of the ECB Supervisory Board (TA-10-2026-0033) and adopted its annual assessment of the ECB (TA-10-2026-0034), alongside a resolution on safeguarding financial stability amid economic uncertainties (TA-10-2026-0004). These measures reflect Parliament's growing role in financial oversight, with MEP oversight intensity reaching 8.54 parliamentary questions per member in 2026 — up from 5.76 in 2004.

Workers' Rights and Social Policy

Two texts advanced the social agenda: the resolution on addressing subcontracting chains to protect workers' rights (TA-10-2026-0050) and the development of a new EU anti-poverty strategy (TA-10-2026-0049). These complement the just transition directive on ensuring job creation while revitalising local economies (TA-10-2026-0003), adopted in the January plenary.

Parliamentary Fragmentation Context

With parliamentary fragmentation at historic highs — the effective number of parties reaching 6.59 and no two-group majority possible since 2019 — every legislative act adopted in February required coalition building across at least three political groups. EP10 has now adopted 56 texts since the start of 2026, maintaining strong legislative momentum. The top-two group concentration (EPP and S&D) stands at approximately 44.5% of seats, down from 63.9% in 2004, making broad coalition building an institutional necessity rather than a political choice.
