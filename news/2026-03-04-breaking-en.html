Breaking: Parliament Marks Four Years of Ukraine War with Defence and Social Policy Push

EP adopts landmark Ukraine resolution alongside strategic defence partnerships, climate neutrality framework, and anti-poverty strategy

⚡ BREAKING — 2026-03-04T15:11:05.762Z

The European Parliament has marked the fourth anniversary of Russia's full-scale invasion of Ukraine with a comprehensive resolution calling for sustained European security commitments. The February plenary sessions also delivered landmark texts on EU strategic defence partnerships, climate neutrality, and a new anti-poverty strategy — reinforcing EP10's capacity for productive multi-group coalition building despite historic parliamentary fragmentation.

Ukraine: Four Years of Solidarity

Adopted on 24 February 2026, the anniversary of Russia's invasion, the resolution on "Four years of Russia's war of aggression against Ukraine" (TA-10-2026-0056) reaffirms Parliament's commitment to Ukrainian sovereignty and calls for continued military, financial, and humanitarian support. This was complemented by the Ukraine Support Loan regulation for 2026-2027 (TA-10-2026-0035) and amendments to the Ukraine Facility (TA-10-2026-0036), establishing a concrete financial framework for sustained assistance.

Defence and Strategic Partnerships

The February session saw the adoption of two pivotal security texts: EU strategic defence and security partnerships (TA-10-2026-0040) and the common security and defence policy annual report (TA-10-2026-0013). Combined with the January resolution on drones and new warfare systems (TA-10-2026-0020), these texts signal an accelerating shift toward European defence autonomy. The multiannual financial framework amendment (TA-10-2026-0037) underpins these ambitions with budgetary commitments.

Climate and Social Policy

Parliament adopted its framework for achieving climate neutrality (TA-10-2026-0031), providing a legal roadmap for the EU's net-zero transition. Social policy received equal attention through a new EU anti-poverty strategy (TA-10-2026-0049) and protections for workers in subcontracting chains (TA-10-2026-0050). These texts demonstrate EP10's ability to maintain the Green Deal legacy while addressing cost-of-living concerns.

Human Rights and Rule of Law

The Parliament continued its human rights advocacy with resolutions on targeted expulsions in Türkiye (TA-10-2026-0047), systemic oppression in Iran (TA-10-2026-0046), the situation in Northeast Syria (TA-10-2026-0053), and the post-election situation in Uganda (TA-10-2026-0045). The immunity waiver for MEP Petr Bystron (TA-10-2026-0027) also demonstrated Parliament's commitment to internal accountability.

Parliamentary Dynamics

With 56 texts adopted in 2026 so far, EP10 is maintaining strong legislative momentum. The fragmented parliament — where no two-party majority is possible and the effective number of parties stands at 6.59 — continues to build ad hoc coalitions across at least three political groups for each legislative majority. EPP, S&D, and Renew remain the core coalition partners, though EPP increasingly cooperates with ECR on defence and migration dossiers.
