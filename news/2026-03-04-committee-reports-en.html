EU Parliament Committee Activity Report: ENVI, ECON, AFET, LIBE, AGRI

Analysis of recent legislative output, effectiveness metrics, and key committee activities

The European Parliament's committee system is driving an intensive legislative agenda this week, with seven recently updated adopted texts (TA-10-2025-0281 through TA-10-2025-0308) signalling continued momentum across environmental, economic, and foreign affairs policy domains. With 30 new plenary reports (A10-0001 through A10-0030) submitted and over 30 ordinary legislative procedures (COD) active in 2025, Parliament's 10th term is maintaining high legislative throughput despite the most fragmented political landscape in EU history.

Environment and Public Health: ENVI Committee Leads on Chemicals and Waste

The ENVI Committee remains at the forefront of Parliament's environmental agenda. The recently adopted text TA-10-2025-0045 on establishing a Common Data Platform on Chemicals and a monitoring and outlook framework represents a significant step toward comprehensive chemicals management across the EU. This legislation creates a centralised infrastructure for tracking chemical substances, their uses, and environmental impact — a direct response to growing concerns about exposure risks and the need for evidence-based regulation.

Equally consequential is TA-10-2025-0167 on the Waste Framework Directive: textiles and food waste, adopted on 9 September 2025. This text establishes new obligations for Member States to reduce textile waste and food loss, aligning with the EU's broader circular economy objectives. The directive introduces extended producer responsibility schemes for textiles and mandatory food waste reduction targets that will reshape supply chains across the internal market.

ENVI's workload intensity is rated HIGH for the current period, with 100 active legislative files and strong legislative output. The committee's focus on chemicals regulation and waste management reflects the EU's commitment to the European Green Deal implementation during the 10th parliamentary term.

Economic and Financial Affairs: ECON Committee Tackles Digital Taxation

The ECON Committee has delivered two landmark texts on tax modernisation. TA-10-2025-0012 on VAT rules for the digital age, adopted on 12 February 2025, overhauls the value-added tax framework to address the challenges posed by platform economies, digital services, and cross-border e-commerce. The reform introduces real-time digital reporting requirements and a single VAT registration system that promises to reduce compliance burdens for businesses operating across multiple Member States.

Complementing this is TA-10-2025-0013 on Administrative cooperation in the field of taxation, also adopted on 12 February, which strengthens information-sharing mechanisms between national tax authorities. Together, these texts form a comprehensive package to modernise EU tax administration for the digital era.

On corporate sustainability, TA-10-2025-0064 amends the timelines for the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) and Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive (CSDDD), postponing certain application dates. This pragmatic adjustment, adopted on 3 April 2025, responds to concerns from businesses about implementation readiness while preserving the fundamental reporting and due diligence obligations.

Foreign Affairs: AFET Committee Addresses Geopolitical Hotspots

The AFET Committee has been particularly active on human rights and neighbourhood policy. TA-10-2025-0004 on the systematic repression of human rights in Iran, specifically the cases of Pakhshan Azizi and Wrisha Moradi, was adopted on 23 January 2025, addressing the taking of EU citizens as hostages. TA-10-2025-0038 on the unlawful detention and sham trials of Armenian hostages by Azerbaijan, including high-ranking political representatives from Nagorno-Karabakh, was adopted on 13 March 2025.

On enlargement policy, Parliament adopted reports on Türkiye (TA-10-2025-0092) and Serbia (TA-10-2025-0093) covering 2023 and 2024, both adopted on 7 May 2025. These reports assess reform progress and EU accession prospects. Additionally, TA-10-2025-0022 on the Reform and Growth Facility for the Republic of Moldova (adopted 11 March 2025) demonstrates Parliament's commitment to supporting democratic transition in the Eastern neighbourhood.

On defence, TA-10-2025-0058 on the implementation of the Common Security and Defence Policy — annual report 2024 (adopted 2 April 2025) charts the course for EU defence integration, a priority area given the EPP-led push for strategic autonomy in the 10th term.

Justice and Home Affairs: LIBE Committee Strengthens Judicial Cooperation

The LIBE Committee has advanced two significant texts on EU judicial cooperation. TA-10-2025-0055 on the EU-Bosnia and Herzegovina Agreement on Eurojust cooperation in criminal matters, adopted on 2 April 2025, extends the EU's judicial cooperation network to the Western Balkans. TA-10-2025-0181 on the extension of the Eurojust case management system timeframe, adopted on 10 September 2025, provides additional time for the implementation of the modernised digital case management infrastructure.

These texts signal continued momentum in strengthening the EU's Area of Freedom, Security and Justice, with a focus on cross-border criminal prosecution capabilities and digital modernisation of judicial systems.

Cross-Committee Legislative Activity

Several adopted texts span multiple committee jurisdictions. TA-10-2025-0109 on modified customs duties for imports from Russia and Belarus (adopted 22 May 2025) reflects coordinated work across INTA and AFET, while TA-10-2025-0177 on amending the ERDF, Cohesion Fund, and Just Transition Fund for mid-term review challenges (adopted 10 September 2025) involves REGI, BUDG, and ENVI committees. The border regions instrument BRIDGEforEU (TA-10-2025-0070, adopted 6 May 2025) further illustrates the cross-committee dimension of cohesion policy.

With 30 plenary reports (A10-0001 through A10-0030) currently active and over 30 new legislative procedures initiated in 2025, Parliament's committee system is operating at full capacity. The pipeline includes a mix of ordinary legislative procedures (COD), consultation procedures (NLE/CNS), and budget procedures (BUD), reflecting the breadth of the legislative agenda.

Why This Matters

The current committee workload reflects the structural dynamics of the 10th parliamentary term. With a fragmentation index of 6.59 — the highest in European Parliament history — and no two-party majority possible, every legislative file requires coalition-building across at least three political groups. The EPP (185 seats, 25.7%), S&D (135, 18.8%), and ECR (84, 11.7%) form the most common winning combinations, though the Renew group (76, 10.6%) and Greens/EFA (53, 7.4%) remain pivotal swing votes on environmental and digital legislation.

The seven recently updated adopted texts in the feed suggest Parliament is accelerating its pace as it approaches the mid-term of EP10. With 114 legislative acts adopted in the current year and committee workload intensity rated HIGH across all five major committees, MEPs are navigating a demanding agenda spanning environmental regulation, tax modernisation, geopolitical positioning, and judicial cooperation. The coming weeks will be critical as several of these texts move to trilogue negotiations with the Council.
