Key Takeaways

  1. All five featured committees show moderate legislative effectiveness (42.4% overall), suggesting room for improvement in amendment drafting and cross-party coalition building.
  2. Committee attendance averaging 61% indicates persistent engagement gaps that may slow legislative throughput and weaken democratic scrutiny.
  3. With 51% amendment success rates across committees, the Parliament demonstrates a balanced but not dominant legislative influence vis-à-vis the Council and Commission.
  4. The concentration of active files (5 per committee) reflects a focused but capacity-constrained legislative programme in the current term.
  5. Cross-committee coordination will be critical as policy files increasingly span multiple domains — particularly at the intersection of ENVI, ECON, and AGRI mandates.
