投票异常 — 情报分析

根据欧洲议会数据:

EP10 Parliamentary Fragmentation Alert: The European Parliament's effective number of political parties has reached 6.50 in 2025, the highest level ever recorded. The Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) has fallen to 0.1538 — confirming a structural shift from a near-duopoly to a multi-polar party system. No two-party majority has been possible since 2019 (EP9). Top-two group concentration (CR2) fell to 45.0%, well below the 50% majority threshold. The minimum winning coalition now requires 3 groups, increasing legislative negotiation complexity. This represents a regime change in EU parliamentary governance.

联盟动态评估

根据欧洲议会数据:

Coalition Dynamics Shift: EP10 political landscape shows a significant rightward rebalancing. The bipolar index shifted from 0.081 (2004) to 0.231 (2025). Eurosceptic and far-right seat share grew from 5.1% in 2004 to 15.4% in 2025, with the most dramatic surge during 2023-2024. EPP leads with 188 seats (26.1%), S&D holds 136 seats (18.9%), PfE has 84 seats (11.7%), ECR at 78 seats (10.8%), Renew at 77 seats (10.7%), and Greens/EFA at 53 seats (7.4%). ESN holds 25 seats (3.5%), GUE/NGL 46 seats (6.4%). Grand coalition (EPP + S&D) holds only 45.0% — insufficient for a majority, requiring at least one additional partner for any legislative initiative.

Analytical Report — EP10 Legislative Outlook: EP10 (2024-2029) began with 720 MEPs across 8 political groups. Legislative productivity historically follows a bell curve within each parliamentary term, peaking in years 3-4. EP9 achieved record legislative output (148 acts in 2023), driven by digital regulation (AI Act, DSA, DMA) and the Green Deal. EP10 signals a pivot toward defence, migration, and industrial policy.
